HOW A CONNECTION BECOMES A CAR · drive-by-wire routing

the websocket is the identity — vehicle_id is just the car's name in the world
The middle box — what a DriveSession is

A DriveSession is a plain Python object (drive_server.py:418), created once per WebSocket connection, living only in the drive server's memory. Think of it as a hotel folio: opened at check-in (connect), it records everything you're assigned and everything you owe — so checkout (disconnect) can settle the room completely.

Identity — minted at connect

Named the instant the socket opens, before any car exists. id(self) is the object's memory address — the session's unique name literally is its Python identity:

self._ego_role = f"ego_vehicle_{id(self):x}"

Possessions — empty until spawn

  • vehicle — live actor handle (the green arrow)
  • _camera_sensor — your streaming camera
  • _perception — the 8-camera / 16-sensor rig
  • scene actors reconstructed for your time window

The "17 sensors · 18 owned" in session_ready is this inventory.

Guards — sanity at 20 Hz

  • _starting — no double-spawn
  • _accepting_frames — a dead session's camera can't write
  • _frame_lock — CARLA's camera thread meets the WebSocket thread here

Verbs — where packets land

Every world-touching message you inject resolves to a method on your session:

  • apply_control() · teleport() · respawn()
  • spawn_object() · set_camera_settings()
  • get_latest_frame() — what the streamer ships you
STAGE 1

Connect

Object created. Exists only in RAM — the world doesn't know you yet.

STAGE 2

start_session

Acquires possessions — car, camera, sensors, scene — all tagged with its role name.

STAGE 3

Drive

Your packets dispatch to this object's methods; telemetry & frames flow back on your socket.

STAGE 4

Disconnect

Walks its stored handles and destroys everything it owns. "Session cleaned up after disconnect."

Why restarts log everyone out: sessions live only in the server process's RAM. New process → no folios → every client must reconnect and spawn again.
Why identity is the connection: it's implemented as "identity is this object" — exactly one per socket, so no packet content can ever reach someone else's car.
